I chose to employ data analytics to answer and visualize this question: is it hotter at the equator? First, I used the Random module in Python to uniformly select Latitudes between -90.000 to 90.000, and Longitudes from -180.000 to 180.000. I generated 2000 unique coordinate pairs. Second, I used the Citipy module to find the nearest city to each coordinate pair, and eliminated any duplicates. This left me with an unbiased sample of 637 cities. Third, I queried the OpenWeatherMap website to get relevant data for each city such as: temperature, % humidity, % cloudiness, and wind speed. Finally, I used pandas and matplotlib to create a DataFrame and visualize the results.
